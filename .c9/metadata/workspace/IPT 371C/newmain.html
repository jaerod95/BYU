IP&T 371B Course Introduction

\n

This course helps to fulfill two requirements for teacher licensure outlined in the Utah Administrative Code which states that the educator preparation program \"includes coursework specifically designed to prepare candidates . . . to teach effectively in traditional, online-only, and blended classrooms [and] to facilitate student use of software for personalized learning\" (UAR R277-504-5.C.3.c-d & 4.A.3.e-f).  The course has four units designed to - 

\n
    \n
  1. Introduce you to concepts in blended and online learning,
    2. \n
  2. Give you experience designing online lessons,
    3. \n
  3. Show you how to facilitate learning online, and
    4. \n
  4. Familiarize you with examples of personalized learning software
    5. \n
\n

 This first lesson is designed to orient you to the course learning environment and help you prepare to participate effectively in your online learning experience.

\n

Complete the Readiness Surveys

\n

These two surveys will help you understand your readiness for being a successful online student and a successful online teacher. Be honest with yourself as you answer the survey questions.  The surveys are diagnostic.  Upon completing each survey, you will receive an email with some feedback on your overall readiness level.  You will not be graded on specific survey responses, but rather on your thoughtful reflection on the feedback you receive from the survey. I hope you will use this information to reflect on how you can focus your learning efforts in the rest of the course.

Complete the Canvas Course Scavenger Hunt

\n

Canvas is a Learning Management System (LMS) like Learning Suite, Blackboard, Moodle, and many others.  LMSs are used to organize online and blended learning environments for the students.  You will be learning to use the Canvas LMS because many of the public school districts in Utah and other states have adopted this tool.  The scavenger hunt link below is a quiz that will introduce you to many of the important features of Canvas.  You can take as much time as you want to complete the scavenger hunt, but you should plan on doing it all in one sitting.  You submit the scavenger hunt twice and your highest score will be recorded in the gradebook.  Remember that the activity is designed to introduce you to important features of the LMS - learning them now will help you to be more successful and efficient in this course.

Organizing IPT371B Professional Learning Communities

\n

As a teacher you will very likely be organized into a professional learning community (PLC).  A PLC is a group of teachers (usually in the same grade level in elementary or subject area in secondary) that collaborate together to improve\n student learning.  They often share curriculum and even collaborate on creating and evaluating common assessments. 

\n

For the next several weeks as you complete Unit 2 of this course, you will have a group of peers who provide feedback to you on the online lesson that you are developing. We will call this group of peers your IPT371 Professional Learning\n Community.  Each student in the course will be responsible for individually designing and creating an online lesson to submit.  The PLC will fulfill the following roles:

\n
    \n
  • PLC members will review the online lesson design of peers in their group
    • \n
  • PLC members will complete assignments and assessments created by peers in their group (this will allow you to practice online grading and providing student feedback online).
    • \n
  • PLC members will provide suggestions for improving the design of the assessments and activities of peers in their group
    • \n
\n\n

Follow these instructions to join a PLC group:

\n
    \n
  • click on the \"People\" link in the lefthand menu 
    • \n
  • click on the Groups tab
    • \n
  • click the \"JOIN\" link for the PLC that you want to join (only four students can join any given group) 
    • \n

Step 6: Provide a Rationale for the 

\n

 It is important for you to think about what value your online/blended activities and assessments add to the lesson.  Share your lesson ideas with at least one member of your PLC and get feedback from him/her on your assignment and activity\n ideas.  Pick one of your assessment ideas and one of your activity ideas to analyze within the planning worksheet using the following criteria:

\n
    \n
  • PIC - What is the student relationship to the technology (Passive, Interactive, Creative)?
    • \n
  • RAT - How does the online lesson change the teacher’s traditional practice (replace, amplify, transform)?
    • \n
  • ACCESS – How does the online lesson affect student access to learning experiences when compared to the traditional lesson?
    • \n
  • STUDENT LEARNING – How does the online lesson intend to impact student learning as compared to the traditional lesson? (i.e., What value does it add?)
    • \n
