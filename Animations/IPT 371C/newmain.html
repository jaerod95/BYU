Organizing IPT371B Professional Learning Communities

As a teacher you will very likely be organized into a professional learning community (PLC).  A PLC is a group of teachers (usually in the same grade level in elementary or subject area in secondary) that collaborate together to improve student learning.  They often share curriculum and even collaborate on creating and evaluating common assessments. 

For the next several weeks as you complete Unit 2 of this course, you will have a group of peers who provide feedback to you on the online lesson that you are developing. We will call this group of peers your IPT371 Professional Learning Community.  Each student in the course will be responsible for individually designing and creating an online lesson to submit.  The PLC will fulfill the following roles:

  • PLC members will review the online lesson design of peers in their group
  • PLC members will complete assignments and assessments created by peers in their group (this will allow you to practice online grading and providing student feedback online).
  • PLC members will provide suggestions for improving the design of the assessments and activities of peers in their group

Follow these instructions to join a PLC group:

  • click on the "People" link in the lefthand menu 
  • click on the Groups tab
  • click the "JOIN" link for the PLC that you want to join (only four students can join any given group)

Step 1: Choose a Learning Objective and Find a Traditional Lesson Plan

Select a CORE curriculum objective from your teaching subject area to focus your lesson on (see http://www.uen.org/core/).  Your design task will be easier if you select a topic

  • that you would currently be comfortable teaching
  • that you have or can find a traditional classroom-based lesson plan for 
  • that lends itself to online activities

As you start thinking about your lesson, you should consider a lesson that can be done within a couple of days to a week's time.  Also, you will want to make sure that there is strong alignment between the outcomes, assessments, and activities that you choose.  Watch this brief video about aligning activities with assessments.

Step 6: Provide a Rationale for the 

 It is important for you to think about what value your online/blended activities and assessments add to the lesson.  Share your lesson ideas with at least one member of your PLC and get feedback from him/her on your assignment and activity ideas.  Pick one of your assessment ideas and one of your activity ideas to analyze within the planning worksheet using the following criteria:

  • PIC - What is the student relationship to the technology (Passive, Interactive, Creative)?
  • RAT - How does the online lesson change the teacher’s traditional practice (replace, amplify, transform)?
  • ACCESS – How does the online lesson affect student access to learning experiences when compared to the traditional lesson?
  • STUDENT LEARNING – How does the online lesson intend to impact student learning as compared to the traditional lesson? (i.e., What value does it add?)

Build Online Module Structure in Canvas LMS

Once you have completed the planning worksheet you will create the structure for your online lesson (or module) in Canvas.  Think of this as your lesson shell - you are creating and organizing the pieces of the lesson, but the content of each of the pieces does not exist yet. 

To complete this assignment do the following:

1. Create a Canvas Course for your module (use the Canvas Guides accessed from the Help menu if needed)

    • Name your course "<first name last name> - Canvas Module with your name in place of "<first name last name>"
    • Add all members of your PLC as "students" in the course and add "charles.graham@byu.edu" as a "Teacher" in your course
    • Make sure your Canvas profile is updated with your name and photo
    • Go to the Canvas settings on the left hand side and familiarize yourself with different options.  Hide all of the navigation elements for your course except for Modules and Grades.
